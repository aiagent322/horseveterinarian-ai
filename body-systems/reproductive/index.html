The Equine Reproductive System

Understanding the mare's reproductive cycle is foundational to successful breeding management. Mares are seasonally polyestrous — they cycle naturally from approximately April through October in the Northern Hemisphere, driven by increasing day length. The transition from winter anestrus to active cycling occurs in spring; the fall transition reverses this.

Mare cycle21-day estrous cycle; behavioral estrus (heat) lasts 5–7 days; ovulation near the end of estrus
SeasonalMares are seasonally polyestrous — naturally cycle spring through fall; transition periods in spring/fall
GestationAverage 340 days; range 320–370 days; considerable individual variation
StallionDaily sperm output correlates with testicular size; semen quality assessable via BSE

EHV-1Most significant infectious reproductive pathogen — causes late-term abortion; vaccinate at months 5, 7, 9

⚠️ Reproductive Emergencies

  • Dystocia — foal not delivered within 30 minutes of amniotic sac rupturing
  • Retained placenta — must be passed within 3 hours of foaling
  • Colic signs in a pregnant mare — uterine torsion possible
  • Excessive hemorrhage after foaling
  • Sudden pregnancy loss at any stage
  • Severe scrotal swelling or pain in a stallion

The Mare's Reproductive Cycle

Understanding the mare's reproductive cycle is foundational to successful breeding management. Mares are seasonally polyestrous — they cycle naturally from approximately April through October in the Northern Hemisphere, driven by increasing day length. The transition from winter anestrus to active cycling occurs in spring; the fall transition reverses this.

During the active breeding season, mares cycle approximately every 21 days. The follicular phase (estrus) lasts 5–7 days and ends with ovulation; the luteal phase (diestrus) lasts approximately 14 days. Breeding is timed to ovulation — typically determined by rectal palpation and ultrasound monitoring of follicle development by an equine reproductive veterinarian.

Artificial Lighting to Advance the Season

  • Mares can be brought into cyclicity earlier by simulating a longer photoperiod starting December 1
  • A 200-watt incandescent (or equivalent LED) light in the stall from approximately 4pm to midnight is the standard protocol
  • Mares typically begin cycling 60–90 days after initiating the artificial lighting program — planned early January start produces February/March cycles
  • Discuss appropriate lighting protocols with your equine reproductive veterinarian

Pregnancy and Parturition

Following successful fertilization, the equine embryo enters the uterus around day 6 and 'fixes' in position around day 17, suppressing the normal luteolytic mechanism. Pregnancy is confirmed by transrectal ultrasound at approximately day 14–16 (detection of embryonic vesicle) and again at day 25–28 (confirmation of heartbeat and single vs. twin pregnancy).

Twin pregnancies are a significant problem in horses — the uterus cannot support two foals to term, and spontaneous abortion or birth of one or both non-viable twins is the most common outcome. Twin reduction performed by the veterinarian before day 30 is the preferred management.

✅ Reproductive Health Planning

  1. Pre-breeding exam 4–6 weeks before the planned breeding date for mares
  2. Annual BSE 4–6 weeks before the breeding season for stallions
  3. Start artificial lighting December 1 if early-season breeding is planned
  4. Pregnancy check at day 14–16 after breeding — confirm conception and rule out twins
  5. EHV vaccination at months 5, 7, 9 for pregnant mares
  6. Pre-foaling vaccine boosters 4–6 weeks before expected foaling date

📋 Discussion Points for Your Reproductive Vet

  • Mare's cycle regularity and any history of reproductive problems
  • Uterine culture and cytology — rule out endometritis before breeding
  • Photoperiod timing and lighting protocol for your facility
  • Twin reduction protocol if twins are detected
  • Breeding method selection — live cover, cooled transported semen, frozen semen — and implications for conception rates
